```
16-week powerbuilding program, 4 days per week, upper/lower split.

Current estimated maxes:
- Squat: 315 lbs
- Bench Press: 225 lbs
- Deadlift: 385 lbs
- Overhead Press: 145 lbs

Strength goals:
- Squat to 345 lbs
- Bench to 250 lbs
- Deadlift to 405 lbs

Weaknesses:
- Weak off the chest on bench press (need more paused bench and chest accessory work)
- Squat tends to turn into a good morning at heavy loads (need quad-dominant accessories and front squats)
- Grip gives out on heavy deadlifts (add grip work and use mixed grip sparingly)
- Lagging rear delts and upper back thickness

Block structure:
- Block 1 (weeks 1-4): Hypertrophy — higher rep ranges (8-12), moderate weight (65-75% of max), 4 working sets per compound, focus on building volume in weak areas
- Block 2 (weeks 5-8): Strength — moderate rep ranges (5-8), heavier weight (75-85%), introduce paused variations for bench and squat
- Block 3 (weeks 9-12): Peaking — lower rep ranges (3-5), heavy weight (85-92%), reduce accessory volume, prioritize the big 3
- Block 4 (week 13-16): Deload + Test — week 13-14 at reduced volume/intensity (50-60%), weeks 15-16 build back up to test new maxes

Additional notes:
- Include rear delt and face pull work at least 3x per week
- Add front squats as a secondary squat variation in blocks 1-2
- Include Romanian deadlifts for posterior chain development
- Keep direct arm work to 2-3 sets per session max, prioritize compounds
- RPE-based loading: working sets should target RPE 7-8 in hypertrophy, RPE 8-9 in strength, RPE 9-10 in peaking
```
